Finance

Finance is also one of Ganga's interest but he feels that this should be taught more heavily in schools around the world. He is passionnate about finance and is constantly learning new concepts and evaluating businesses to invest in. His first year in the stock market, he made a 60+% return on his money. He was able to accomplish this though 2 strategies, long term growth investing and swing trading. Long term growth investing is investing in a growth company such as Tesla and Facebook. Swing trading is more riskier than long term growth investing but it is the idea of purchasing a stock for the purpose of selling in a week to a couple months. The main focus for Ganga when swing trading is finding stocks that over react to news, such as AMD, and purchase those when prices drop and sell in a short time frame for profits around 5 to 12%.

Tips

Fully understand the business

Read the 10K and 10Q for the company

Listen to conference calls

Understand the risk factors

Look for positive net income

Look for PE ratio under 20

Look for forward PE under 15

Look for growing revenues

Look for high investment to debt ratio

Look at the track record for the company, CEO, and the management team

Do you feel comfortable holding this stock for the next 5 years?

How would you react if the price were to drop drastically tomorrow?
